  • EMPATHY

    A basic prerequisite for being plant-conscious is developing empathy for plants. You could see empathy as a bridge for two life forms to start exchanging valuable information in a proper manner. I bet you would learn more from a coach you respect deeply, than from a person you dislike. You should be educated and personally in touch with the subject, as you cannot develop empathy for something you do not encounter in daily life.

    It is a hopeful fact that (plant) empathy is a skill that can be learned. It can even be exercised by simple acts like keeping houseplants, which is proven to improve your overall wellbeing. Other methods of developing empathy for plants are gardening and visiting forests, botanical gardens or nurseries. Seek for other plant enthusiasts and spread the love!

  • SCIENCE

    The most reliable tool we own is science. Our knowledge of plants is evolving rapidly. We are starting to discover how complex and strategic plants are.

    Experiments on individual specimens conclude that plants can probably see, hear, feel, remember, count, talk, and even calculate probability in some way. These abilities are, of course, personified; they do not operate with the same mechanisms as humans do. This growing field of research motivated scientists to talk, provocatively, about plant neurology. Which, is, of course, an oxymoron given that plants have no nervous system.

    We now look at plants with more knowledge, increasingly discovering the inherent nature of that life form. And in turn, this new knowledge challenges us to scrutinise our understanding of plant science as a whole.

  • PHILOSOPHY

    As much as we would like to know certain facts, some things will remain a mystery. Science has a hard time proving some truths, by example emotional, ethical or existential values. For these topics and other unexplained subjects, we use philosophy to dig deeper.

    We search for patterns and logical links, while listening carefully to each other. Our discussions are largely fuelled by our intuitive thoughts on plants, personal experience and top-notch plant consciousness information coming from authors, botanists and recent studies.

  • DURABILITY

    While working closely with plants, we strive to develop strategies for a green future. Ecology is getting more important by the day. With the ability to grow just with the energy of the sun, plants are at the base of every animal that lives on Earth. They cleanse the soil, purify the air and provide food, medicine, building materials and so much more.

    Currently, one thirth of all plant species are vulnerable and loss of ecosystems is a major problem worldwide. We are seeing a worldwide switch from wild organisms to cultivated species. This evolution seems inevitable in our modern society, yet we can make this switch in a mindful way. We are convinced that humans can create a durable future with healthy agriculture, healthy wildlife like bees and butterflies and safely keep endangered species.

  • THE ART OF GARDENING

    Gardening is an act of love. It works therapeutical, antidepressant and it is a natural form of fitness. Gardening combines knowledge and intuition. One raises plants in a unique microclimate, together with other plants, animals and fungi. The soil is the base where fungi can make the impossible possible by their sometimes kilometre-long range. The gardener stands at the forefront of decision making concerning plant combinations regarding geometrical shapes, (flower) colours, heights and vitality.

    With every act of work, the utopian garden in his imaginative mind and his real garden come a little closer to each other. This can result in a garden containing some of the most beautiful art, inspiring even the most famous painters.
